The Index Framework

Understanding how the W4C Index works

Core Realms of Water Sensitivity

The W4C Index is structured around four interconnected realms, crucial for delivering sustainable urban water management:

Technology

Assessing existing infrastructure, promoting efficient tools and models, and fostering continuous innovation.

  • Resilience to flooding
  • SCADA systems
  • Wastewater recycling

Environment

Understanding nature-built form interactions, integrating water-sensitive changes, and managing urban green-blue spaces.

  • Aqua Health Index
  • Water circularity
  • Green-blue spaces

Society

Evaluating public awareness, social vulnerability, community satisfaction, and equitable access to water.

  • Water literacy
  • Disaster preparedness
  • Community engagement

Governance

Aligning strategic visions, mediating across boundaries, and ensuring adaptive management.

  • Early warning systems
  • Citizen engagement
  • Policy incentives

The 26 Indicators

A comprehensive set of 26 indicators forms the backbone of the W4C Index, meticulously selected and refined through stakeholder collaboration.

These indicators cover aspects from infrastructure robustness and intelligent water control to the health of water bodies, public awareness, and interdepartmental coordination.

Scoring and Interpretation

The W4C Index employs a hybrid scoring system:

  • Indicators within Technology, Environment, and Society are rated quantitatively (scale of 1-5) using newly developed formulas.
  • Governance indicators are assessed qualitatively (0 or 1) based on structured questionnaires.

This approach allows for local contextual interpretations while maintaining a robust foundation for comparing and benchmarking cities.

Realm Scores are calculated by averaging the scores of their respective indicators, providing a clear snapshot of performance.
