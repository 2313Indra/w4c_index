Development & Methodology

Understanding our rigorous, multi-phase development process

A Rigorous, Multi-Phase Process (2019-2024)

The W4C Index was developed over five years through a structured, iterative process, guided by an international collaboration between India and the Netherlands (Water4Change Project - https://w4cproject.org/home/).

Citizen Science & Participatory Approach

At the heart of the W4C Index is a commitment to citizen science and co-creation.

Three series of workshops were conducted in Bhopal, Bhuj, and Kozhikode, involving a diverse group of stakeholders: mid-level city officials, end-users, NGOs, civil society, academics, and community members.

Workshop Phases

Framing the Transition Challenge

Identifying key water challenges and root causes using problem tree analysis.

Water-Sensitive Cities Visioning

Co-creating a shared vision for a water-sensitive future.

Pathways & Experiments

Defining practical steps and refining indicators for action.

Case Study Cities: Our Learning Grounds

The W4C Index was refined from the stakeholders contribution in three diverse Indian cities, each presenting unique water management challenges:

Bhopal

  • Urban lakes
  • Monsoon dependence
  • Groundwater concerns

Bhuj

  • Arid climate
  • Historical water systems
  • Groundwater depletion

Kozhikode

  • Coastal city
  • High rainfall
  • Flooding
  • Stormwater management

Insights from these cities were invaluable in shaping a versatile and adaptable index.
