About the W4C Index

Understanding our mission to transform water management in Indian cities

What is the W4C Index?

The Water4Change (W4C) Index is a novel, comprehensive tool designed to evaluate a city's water sensitivity and improve sustainable water resource management in India. It addresses the critical need for a robust index tailored to the diverse regional contexts of the country.

It empowers stakeholders to gain early insights into water crises and track progress towards achieving Water-Sensitive City (WSC) goals.

The Challenge: Why India Needs the W4C Index

Indian cities face mounting pressure on water resources due to rapid growth, climate change, and infrastructure gaps, especially in secondary cities.

Existing indices, like the state-level CWMI, are not designed to assess the nuanced water sensitivity of individual urban areas. Global indices often lack contextual relevance or overlook crucial social and governance aspects vital for India.

There's a clear gap in measures for nature-based solutions, green/grey infrastructure, and the cumulative effects of urban runoff â€“ all critical for water-sensitive performance.

Our Vision: Towards Water-Sensitive Indian Cities

A Water-Sensitive City (WSC) ensures water security, optimizes diverse resources, preserves waterway health, mitigates flood risks, and creates public spaces that collect, clean, and recycle water.

The W4C Index is built on the vision of co-creating fit-for-purpose guidelines that account for site-specific issues, knowledge, and practices in Indian secondary cities.

What Makes W4C Unique?

Citizen Science Driven

Deeply rooted in public participation and local knowledge, crucial for India's diverse socio-cultural landscape.

Context-Specific for India

Developed with and for Indian cities, addressing unique challenges like unplanned construction and data variability.

Holistic (TESG Realms)

Integrates Technology, Environment, Society, and Governance for a balanced assessment.

Co-Created Framework

Developed through intensive workshops with diverse stakeholders across Bhopal, Bhuj, and Kozhikode.

Hybrid Scoring

Combines quantitative data with qualitative, expert-driven assessments, making it practical for varying data availability.
